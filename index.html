Dr. Maya Angelou

Maya Angelou was a world-famous author. She was best known for her unique and pioneering autobiographical writing style.
A timeline of Dr. Maya Angelou's life.
  • 1928Maya Angelou is born Marguerite Annie Johnson in St. Louis, Missouri.
  • 1931Maya Angelou’s parents divorced, and she is sent to live in Arkansas with her grandmother.
  • 1935Maya Angelou returns to live with her mother. She faces a period of mutism.
  • 1936Maya Angelou returns to Arkansas and her grandmother’s care.
  • 1936Maya Angelou’s teacher Bertha Flowers helps introduce her to literature.
  • 1042Maya Angelou returns to live with her mother.
  • 1942Maya Angelou and her family relocated to Oakland, California.
  • 1944Maya Angelou becomes the first Black woman to be a streetcar conductor in San Francisco.
  • 1945Maya Angelou’s son Clyde is born.
  • 1951Maya Angelou marries Tosh Angelos. The family relocated to New York.
  • 1954Maya Angelou and her husband divorced. Maya Angelou begins performing in San Francisco.
  • 1955Maya Angelou tours Europe performing in operas.
  • 1959Maya Angelou meets John Oliver Killens who encourages her to pursue her writing career in New York.
  • 1959Maya Angelou moves to New York and joins the Harlem Writer’s Guild.
  • 1960Maya Angelou fundraises for the civil rights movement with Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.
  • 1961Maya Angelou moves to Cairo, Egypt.
  • 1962Maya Angelou moves to Accra, Ghana, where she meets Malcolm X.
  • 1965Maya Angelou returns to the United States; Malcolm X is assassinated.
  • 1965 - 1968Maya Angelou travels between Los Angeles, Hawaii, and New York.
  • 1968Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. is assassinated.
  • 1969Maya Angelou publishes “I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings”
  • 1970 - 1975Maya Angelou continues to publish more work.
  • 1972Maya Angelou is nominated for a Pulitzer Prize for her poetry collection “Just Give Me a Cool Drink of Water 'fore I Diiie”
  • 1973Maya Angelou marries Paul de Feu.
  • 1975Maya Angelou is appointed to the American Revolution Bicentennial Council.
  • 1977Maya Angelou is appointed to the presidential commission for International Women’s Year.
  • 1978Maya Angelou publishes “And Still I Rise”
  • 1981Maya Angelou and her husband divorced.
  • 1981Maya Angelou is awarded a lifetime appointment as Reynolds Professorship of American Studies at Wake Forest University.
  • 1993Maya Angelou performs at the inauguration of President Bill Clinton.
  • 1998Maya Angelou is inducted into the National Women’s Hall of Fame.
  • 2000Maya Angelou is awarded the National Medal of Arts by President Bill Clinton.
  • 2000 - 2008Maya Angelou publishes more biographies and poetry and works in the entertainment field.
  • 2008Maya Angelou endorses Hillary Clinton, then Barack Obama, for president.
  • 2010Maya Angelou is awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Barack Obama.
  • 2013Maya Angelou publishes her eighth autobiography “Mom & Me & Mom.”
  • 2014Maya Angelou passes away at the age of 86.
  • 2021Maya Angelou becomes the first Black woman to be depicted on a quarter.
